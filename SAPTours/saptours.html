SAPTours

Overview

This past summer, a friend asked me to design a web application with him for our school's admissions office that would allow college tour guides to effectively schedule tours. It is now currently in use.

Role: UX/UI Designer

Date: May - December, 2019

Tools: Adobe XD, Adobe Illustrator

Background

My friend is a student tour guide for our school. At the beginning of each semester, the coordinator team releases the potential slots of tours. Guides are also given a calendar created by the coordinators at the beginning of the month. Tour guides enter their availability on a sheet, and then coordinators are responsible for randomizing which tour guides give a tour every single month. This process ended up being inefficient, messy, and confusing. My friend therefore pitched the idea of creating an app that would allow them to efficiently schedule tours, and asked me to be the UX/UI designer.

The Challenge

Design a web app that would make scheduling tours easy and efficient. And can be coded by my partner.

My friend first showed me their old scheduling process. The tour guides shared a giant Excel sheet that contained everyone's information and schedules, and any upcoming tours. Through the Excel sheet, guides picked up any tours that were available. With so much unorganized information in one place, it was tedious to navigate and extremely difficult to keep track of what other guides were doing. As a result, he received approval from the Admissions Office to design an app that would streamline the tour-scheduling process.

The Process

Before we could think about how we wanted the app to work, we first had to think about what kind of app we wanted. We had three options: web app, mobile app, and automation process. We analyzed the pros and cons of each platform.

Ultimately, we chose to create a web app as we felt it would offer the most utility. My friend would code it using React. From there, we brainstormed necessary features and consulted with the lead tour guide and guide supervisor. We defined two types of users and their functionalities:

From there, we came up with other features:

1. Admins can add tour times, along with how many tours are available.

2. Tour guides would add in their availability for the tour times created. They could edit this availability at any time. They could also set a minimum number of tours.

3. Admins could see a list of active/inactive tour guides at any time. Only admins could inactivate a tour guide.

4. E-mails would be sent out 24 hours before tours to remind students.

5. If a tour guide drops a tour, an e-mail would be sent out to all tour guides with that time marked as available. They could them claim the tour, and it would be assigned to them.

6. List of tour guides with searchable majors/potentially clubs, to help see if a guide is a good match for a specific tour group (ex. a guide who is an international student could be a better fit for a group of prospective international students).

Since it's a scheduling app, we decided a calendar-based app was the way to go. I researched other calendar apps, such as Google Calendar, for inspiration. I also looked into numerous social media apps, such as Facebook and Instagram, for the tour guide profiles.

The Result

We decided to call our app SAPTours (Student Ambassador Program Tours). My friend finished coding it and released it in December 2019. It is now currently being used by our school's admissions office. The logo, a college building, was created by me using Illustrator. The app's colors, blue and white, are our school's colors.

Due to technical limitations and differing opinions, the app does not completely match my vision, but it has the general idea. Below is my vision, which is from the point of view of a guide since the admin features were created in the backend. Here are screenshots of the real app.



Login page. I liked Facebook's straightforward approach to logging in and creating an account, displaying both on the same page. New/current users know immediately where to go. To ensure only Brandeis students can access it, the app checks for a Brandeis email. When signing up, users must indicate their roles.



The home page. It's set up to look like a calendar, making it intuitive to use. Any event for that month will be shown on its date. There is also a legend at the top for reference, and users have the option to only view their own tours. Users have the option download the calendar to their Google Calendar so they have a copy of it. There is a menu on the top right-hand corner.



Covering a tour. Users can click on each event and find information about it. They can then choose to cover the tour.


The guide list. All users can see the full list of guides, along with the guide's information. Users can search for specific types of guides. They can also email them directly. These features can be helpful for matching a guide with a tour group or event.




Profile page. Users fill out all their information here. The information is organized into different categories, and users can see clearly which category they're at. They can change their profile photo and view their tour stats as well.

Reflection

This project was a great opportunity for me because it's an app that was actually going to be put into use. It was also my first time working with a developer, so I learned that I sometimes needed to compromise to accommodate to their abilities. Since this app is currently being used, my friend and I can see that users are able to learn it easily and efficiently schedule tours. Moving forward, we may need to update the app to support any future needs. There were also some features we were unable to implement but would like to one day, such as the analytics feature shown in my wireframe.
