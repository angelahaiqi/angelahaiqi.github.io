It all started with animated films. I always loved animals, and cartoons would bring them to life. Inspired, I began drawing animals of my own. This led to me borrowing art books from the library, to visiting all the museums and galleries in NYC, to teaching myself design software. I loved creating, but was told to pursue other fields. Growing up in a time where technology evolved so rapidly, I chose to study Computer Science at Brandeis University. I was fascinated by technology's potential to solve problems and move society forward. I also studied Economics, as I was drawn to its core of thinking about complex subjects from different perspectives. To the shock of many, I picked up a third major - East Asian Studies - purely because I was and still am a huge Chinese history nerd.

Although I loved my classes and thought I was going to be a software engineer, I still did graphic/web design on the side. Then, my junior year of undergrad, I learned about UX/UI design and knew immediately that was what I wanted to do. Ultimately, I want to help others, and design provides the genuine empathy and creativity that technology needs to do so. Since then, I've been on a self-motivated journey where I've expanded my design skills and thinking through various projects and internships.

Currently, I'm working as a UX/UI Designer for NESN, the New England Sports Network. I am helping to redesign and expand its ever-growing digital presence which impacts hundreds of thousands of people across the country. I'm always looking to connect with others, so feel free to reach out for collaborations or just chat! :)
